Dr. Pratima Lamichhane (MBBS, KU-LMC, RN)

Health Program Coordinator at SGMP

It is with immense pleasure and great honor that we extend a warm welcome to Dr. Pratima Lamichhane as the Health Program Coordinator at SGMP. Dr. Lamichhane brings with her a wealth of experience and expertise in the realm of healthcare, having dedicated a significant portion of her career to the curative field. Her journey has been marked by a profound commitment to the well-being and care of individuals, a testament to her unwavering dedication to the medical profession.

Among her many accomplishments, Dr. Lamichhane has garnered exceptional exposure in the curative domain, particularly during her tenure at the prestigious Teaching Hospital in Kathmandu, where she served in the cardiac department. Her time there not only honed her skills but also provided her with invaluable insights into the complexities of patient care and treatment. We are confident that her extensive experience and compassionate approach will greatly enrich our health programs and contribute to the continued success of SGMP.

Once again, we are thrilled to have Dr. Pratima Lamichhane join our team and look forward to the positive impact she will undoubtedly make in her new role.

Personal Information

On the 18th of February, in the serene town of Ratnanagar-01, nestled in the heart of Chitwan, a new life began its journey. This individual, a proud Nepalese by nationality, carries the rich cultural heritage and traditions of Nepal. Fluent in both Nepali and English, they navigate the world with a unique blend of local wisdom and global perspective, bridging cultures and communities through the power of language.

Academic Qualification

With a steadfast commitment to academic excellence and professional growth, my educational journey has been marked by consistent achievement and dedication. I began my formal education at Chitrawan English Boarding School in Chitwan, where I earned my School Leaving Certificate (S.L.C.) in 2010, securing a First Division. Building on this foundation, I pursued a passion for healthcare by completing my Proficiency Certificate Level (PCL) in Nursing from Kathmandu Nursing Campus between 2010 and 2013, graduating with First Division and Distinction. This was followed by my successful registration with the Nepal Nursing Council (N.N.C.) in 2014, where I obtained permanent registration (No. 24647).

Seeking to broaden my academic horizons, I completed my Higher Secondary Education (H.S.E.B.) from Chitwan Higher Secondary School in 2015, again achieving a First Division. Driven by a deeper aspiration to serve in the medical field, I embarked on the rigorous journey of earning my Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) from Lumbini Medical College in Palpa, affiliated with Kathmandu University, which I completed in 2022 with a First Division. Most recently, in January 2023, I successfully passed the Nepal Medical Council (N.M.C.) licensing examination, earning my provisional registration (No. 31427). Each milestone in my academic and professional path reflects not only my dedication to learning but also my unwavering commitment to contributing meaningfully to the field of healthcare.

Work Journey and Experiences

With a rich tapestry of professional experiences spanning nearly a decade, I have cultivated a diverse and profound expertise in the medical field. My journey began as the Nursing In-Charge in the Psychiatry Department at CMC, where I served diligently from June 1, 2014, to November 11, 2015. This role not only honed my leadership skills but also deepened my understanding of mental health care. Following this, I embarked on an enriching Internship Programme at LMC, which spanned from May 30, 2022, to May 29, 2023. This period was instrumental in broadening my clinical acumen and reinforcing my commitment to patient-centered care. Currently, I am contributing my skills and knowledge at the prestigious Manmohan Cardiothoracic and Transplant Center in Kathmandu, affiliated with Tribhuvan University, where I continue to thrive in a dynamic and challenging environment.

My key skills encompass a wide array of emergency and general medical procedures, reflecting my versatility and proficiency in critical care. I am adept in emergency interventions such as Endotracheal Intubation, CPR, Poisoning Management, and general surgical techniques. My experience extends to specialized care in CCU and ICU settings, as well as proficiency in Cath lab procedures. Additionally, I am skilled in general medical procedures including Thoracocentesis, Paracentesis, Catheterisation, Lumbar Puncture, and Central Venous Pressure (CVP) monitoring. These competencies underscore my ability to deliver high-quality care across diverse medical scenarios, ensuring optimal patient outcomes.

Currently, I serve as a Health Program Coordinator at Shahid Ganesh Memorial Pratisthan in Karnali Province, Nepal, a role I began on August 3, 2023. These roles have allowed me to expand my expertise in medical planning, execution, and community healthcare delivery. My journey has been enriched by my ability to adapt, learn, and innovate, making me a well-rounded medical professional dedicated to improving healthcare outcomes.

Trainings and Achievements

With a robust foundation in both clinical practice and academic instruction, I have honed my expertise in diagnosing and managing a wide array of common medical conditions, as well as navigating the complexities of critical care in intensive care unit (ICU) settings. My experience extends to undergraduate teaching, where I have facilitated bedside learning, fostering the next generation of medical professionals. Complementing my clinical and educational roles, I possess strong leadership and public speaking skills, which have been instrumental in guiding teams and delivering impactful presentations.

I have also undergone comprehensive vocational training, equipping me with specialized skills in managing postpartum hemorrhage (PPH), neonatal resuscitation through the Helping Babies Breathe initiative, and infection control practices such as hand hygiene. Additionally, I have organized and led numerous workshops, further contributing to the professional development of my peers. My commitment to community health is reflected in my participation in medical camps, such as those conducted in NJS and Ruru VDC in collaboration with LMC and other organizations, where I provided essential healthcare services to underserved populations.

To stay at the forefront of medical knowledge, I have attended continuing medical education (CME) sessions on critical topics including hypertension, depression, effective communication strategies for delivering difficult news, and advanced nutritional support techniques such as total enteral and parenteral nutrition. My dedication to public health is further underscored by my involvement in various social service initiatives in rural areas, aimed at improving healthcare access and outcomes.

Furthermore, I have completed WHO-endorsed training programs focused on emerging respiratory viruses, including COVID-19, covering detection, prevention, response, and control measures, as well as infection prevention and control (IPC) protocols specific to the novel coronavirus. These experiences have not only enriched my clinical acumen but also reinforced my commitment to addressing global health challenges through education, leadership, and community engagement.
